End-to-End Workflow

1

Open and Classify Every Email

The system monitors the Coast to Coast Carriers inbox, opens each email, reads the body and attachments, and decides which workflow should run.

Statement Insurance CSV Other Email Invoice
2

Index Shipments From TAI Webhooks

TAI POSTs full shipment payloads to us on create, update, and status events. Every reference number on the shipment and its stops is mapped to the TAI shipmentId so invoices can be matched later.

Because carrier invoices only carry text references (load #, PRO, BOL), this index is what lets us resolve them to TAI's numeric shipment ID.
3

Extract Invoice Data

If the email is an invoice, AI extracts the data needed to update TAI and validate billing.

Carrier Invoice Number Invoice Amount Due Date BOL PRO Number POD Added Charges
4

Resolve the TAI Shipment

The system looks up the PRO first, then the load number, in the webhook-built index to find the TAI shipmentId.

If no shipment is indexed: pause as tai_shipment_not_found and notify, since the shipment webhook may not have arrived yet or the reference does not match TAI.
If already completed: the workflow stops with ALREADY_COMPLETED so the customer is never double-invoiced.
5

Check Charges

Before touching the amount, the system screens the extracted charges.

  • If there are unrecognized charges, stop and fail for human review.
  • If extra charges are present with no proof attached, stop and fail for human review.
  • Added charges that do have proof are deducted from the invoice total before the base amount is checked.
6

Validate Invoice Amount Against TAI

The system compares the base carrier amount (invoice total minus any approved added charges) against the TAI carrier cost (buy).

If within ±$0.50 or 2%: the amount is accepted and the workflow continues.
If it does not match: mark the invoice UNMATCHED_AMOUNT and stop for review.
7

Write PRO and Invoice Number to TAI

The carrier PRO number is added as a LinehaulCarrierProNumber reference, and the carrier invoice number is recorded on the shipment. This step is idempotent — it skips the write if the PRO already matches.

8

Mark Delivered and Upload the POD

The delivery stop is updated with the actual arrival and departure times and the POD signed-by name, then the POD document is uploaded to the TAI shipment.

  • If the shipment is already delivered, the step is treated as complete.
  • If there is no delivery stop, the step is safely skipped.
  • The POD is extracted from the carrier invoice PDF and attached to the shipment.
9

Approve the Carrier Bill

The carrier bill is approved in TAI accounting using the invoice number, amount, due date, and the shipment's transit leg type. This step is idempotent — an already-approved matching bill is reused instead of duplicated.

10

Validate Customer, Rate, and Margin

Before customer billing, the system confirms the load is billable and profitable using the TAI customer (sell) rate.

  • If the customer name contains test, pause for manual confirmation.
  • If TAI has no customer rate, pause as needs_customer_rate_review.
  • If profit is below $10, pause for a rate update with a low-margin notice.
  • If customer, rate, and margin all pass, continue to invoicing.
11

Generate the Customer Invoice in TAI

The system creates and approves the customer invoice for the shipment. This is idempotent: an existing invoice is reused, and its total is sanity-checked against the expected customer rate before proceeding.

12

Send the Customer Invoice

The system builds a clean customer invoice PDF and emails it to the customer.

  • Attach the POD extracted from the carrier invoice.
  • Include the customer invoice PDF generated from TAI.
  • Keep all extracted carrier invoice details and step results on the load for audit and review.

Manual Review and Pauses

TAI Shipment Not Found

The invoice could not be matched to a TAI shipment yet. The workflow pauses until the shipment webhook arrives or the reference is corrected.

Unrecognized Charges

The invoice carries charges the system cannot identify. Processing stops and the invoice is failed for human review.

Charges Need Proof

Extra charges are present without supporting proof attached, so the invoice is held and failed for review.

Extra Charges Pending Review

Added charges were verified against proof but are never auto-invoiced — they are held for human approval.

Unmatched Amount

The invoice amount does not match the TAI carrier cost within tolerance and cannot be cleared automatically.

Test Customer

The shipment's customer name contains "test", so the workflow pauses for manual confirmation before billing.

Needs Customer Rate Review

No customer rate is on the TAI shipment, or the profit is too low. The workflow pauses for a rate update.

Invoice Generation Failed

The TAI customer invoice could not be created or its total did not match the expected rate. The workflow pauses for review.

Already Completed

The invoice was already fully processed. The workflow stops to prevent double-invoicing the customer.

Business Value

This workflow keeps email handling, TAI shipment updates, exception routing, and customer invoicing in one automated process for Coast to Coast Carriers. Employees only review the invoices that need judgment, while clean invoices move through to the customer with the POD attached — every step logged and idempotent so a re-run never duplicates work.
