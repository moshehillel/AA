A backend automation system that opens carrier emails, classifies attachments, updates Primus shipments, validates invoice amounts, routes exceptions, and sends clean customer invoices with the POD attached.

End-to-End Workflow

1

Open and Classify Every Email

The system monitors the carrier inbox, opens each email, reads the body and attachments, and decides which workflow should run.

Statement Insurance CSV Other Email Invoice
2

Handle Statements

If the email is a statement, upload it to the proper statement location and complete the email without starting invoice billing.

3

Apply Monthly Insurance CSV

If the attachment is the CSV file for last month's insurance pricing, the system reads every row and adds the insurance cost to each matching BOL number.

This keeps shipment costs updated in Primus before customer billing runs.
4

Route Other Emails Through AI

For non-invoice emails, the AI agent checks whether there are rules for how to answer or process the message.

If no rule matches: send the email to manual review so an employee can decide what to do.
5

Extract Invoice Data

If the email is an invoice, AI extracts the data needed to update Primus and validate billing.

Carrier Invoice Number Invoice Amount Due Date BOL PRO Number POD Added Charges
6

Find and Update the Primus Shipment

The system matches the invoice to the Primus load and enters every shipment detail found on the bill before any billing or review forwarding.

  • Add or confirm carrier name, shipment references, BOL, PRO number, invoice number, due date, and invoice notes.
  • Mark the shipment delivered once the invoice is received.
  • Extract the POD from the invoice and upload it to Primus.
If the shipment cannot be found: forward the email to manual review.
If the shipment is already invoiced: ignore the email and stop the workflow.
7

Validate Customer, Rate, and Profit

Before amount validation and customer billing, the system confirms the load is billable and has enough profit.

  • If no customer is entered, create a Missing Customer task.
  • If no customer rate exists, create a Missing Rate task.
  • If profit is less than $10, create a missing or incorrect rate task.
  • If profit is less than 10%, adjust the commission and add (not verified if possible).
  • If customer, rate, and profit are good, continue to invoice amount validation.
8

Apply Drayage Rules

If the invoice is for a drayage shipment, the workflow follows the special drayage entry rules for that shipment style before continuing.

9

Validate Added Charges

The system checks for lumper, detention, and other accessorial charges before deciding whether the invoice amount matches.

  • If added charges exist, deduct the added charge amount from the invoice total before checking the base carrier amount in Primus.
  • If the base amount is correct after the deduction, create a review task labeled with the charge type, such as Added - Lumper Amount Correct.
  • Make sure the load already has all bill details entered before forwarding the email for review.
  • Forward the email to the correct address for that added-charge review.
10

Validate Invoice Amount Against Primus

If there are no added charges, the system compares the carrier invoice amount directly against Primus. If added charges were already deducted, it compares the adjusted base amount.

If the amount does not match: label the email No Match and forward it to the correct department.
If the amount matches: continue to customer invoicing.
11

Send the Customer Invoice

If customer, rate, profit, shipment details, POD, and invoice amount all pass validation, the system sends the invoice to the customer.

  • Attach the POD extracted from the carrier invoice.
  • Include the customer invoice generated from Primus.
  • Keep all extracted carrier invoice details on the load for audit and review.
12

Monthly Uninvoiced Load Check

Once a month, the system checks all delivered loads that are still not invoiced and requests the missing bill from the carrier.

This catches loads where the carrier never sent the bill and prevents old uninvoiced shipments from being forgotten.

Manual Review and Labels

Manual Review

Created when a general email has no matching AI reply rule or the system cannot decide the next step safely.

Missing Customer

Created when the matched Primus load does not have a customer entered.

Missing Rate

Created when there is no rate, profit is under $10, or the rate appears incorrect.

Added Charge Review

Created for lumper, detention, or other accessorials when the base invoice amount is correct after deducting the added charge.

No Match

Label used when the carrier invoice amount does not match Primus and cannot be cleared automatically.

Shipment Not Found

Used when the invoice cannot be confidently matched to a Primus shipment or BOL. The email is forwarded to manual review.

Already Invoiced

If the matched shipment was already invoiced, the system ignores the email and stops processing.

Business Value

This workflow keeps email handling, Primus updates, exception routing, and customer invoicing in one automated process. Employees only review emails and invoices that need judgment, while clean invoices move through to the customer with the POD attached.
